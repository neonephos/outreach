# NeoNephos Tagline

## "Community-driven open cloud-native stack for European sovereignty."

This is the NeoNephos tagline. It's a single line that captures what the foundation stands for and what it is working toward for first impression.

---

## What it means

- **Community-driven** _ NeoNephos does not build the ecosystem. It enables the community to build it.
- **Open** _ all projects hosted under NeoNephos are open source.
- **Cloud-native stack** _ a modular, interoperable set of building blocks, not a single product.
- **European sovereignty** _ the foundation exists to reduce dependency on non-European infrastructure and vendor lock-in.

---

## A note on two parts of the tagline

Two parts deserve a brief explanation for anyone communicating about NeoNephos externally.

**"Stack"**
NeoNephos is not a fully developed stack yet. That is the goal, not the current state. The projects hosted under NeoNephos together form the building blocks of a future interoperable cloud-native stack – but the work is ongoing and the stack is still taking shape. When using the tagline, be honest about this: we are building toward it, not there yet.

**"European"**
NeoNephos was born out of a European initiative – specifically the vision of reducing dependency on non-European cloud infrastructure. But NeoNephos is open source, which means anyone, anywhere can use it, contribute to it, and benefit from it. "European" describes where the idea came from and the political and strategic context that shaped the foundation, but it's not a geographic restriction on who can participate.

Both of these nuances matter when representing NeoNephos at events, in conversations, or in writing. The tagline is a shorthand, not a complete description. Use it confidently and explain the context behind it when asked.

The tagline appears consistently across all NeoNephos touchpoints:

- **Merchandise** _ on stickers, shirts, and other community swag
- **Social media** _ as a consistent reference point in posts and bios

---

## How to use it

Use the tagline as written. Do not shorten, paraphrase, or adapt it for individual contexts.

If a shorter reference is needed, "open cloud-native stack for European sovereignty" is acceptable. The full version is always preferred.
