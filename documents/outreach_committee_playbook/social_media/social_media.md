# Social Media Strategy General Conditions & Rules

This document describes the general rules and processes governing the NeoNephos Social Media Strategy.

> [!NOTE]
> Each NeoNephos Social Media Strategy plan adheres to the rules and processes set out in this document.

## Mission Statement

> The goal of the NeoNephos Social Media Strategy is to create a framework to optimally support the strategic NeoNephos goals set by the NeoNephos Governing Board through the use of social media and related outlets.

The Social Media Strategy's scope does not include drafting the *overall* strategic vision of NeoNephos.

## Responsibility

The responsibility of creation, modification and maintenance of any NeoNephos Social Media Document is bestowed upon the [Chairperson of the Outreach Committee's Social Media SIG](https://github.com/neonephos/outreach/tree/main/topics/social_media).

## Amendment

This document may be changed by simple majority in the [Social Media SIG](https://github.com/neonephos/outreach/tree/main/topics/social_media) or simple majority in the Outreach Committee.

## Definitions

### NeoNephos Social Media Strategy

The NeoNephos Social Media Strategy minimally consists of:

1. The Social Media Strategy Document (valid for the indicated timeline).
2. The Social Media Strategy Operations Document.
3. Related supplemental information, as indicated in this document.

## Social Media Strategy Document

> The Social Media Strategy Document describes the concrete implementation of the NeoNephos Social Media Strategy for a given timeframe. It is always scoped and time bounded in nature. If the timelines of two Social Media Strategy Documents overlap, the newer document takes precedence. It is developed by the Outreach Committe's Social Media SIG.

> [!Warning]
> A Social Media Strategy Document is never unbounded in duration. Typically its window of validity is aligned with the strategic roadmap of the Governing Board.

### Minimum Elements of a Social Media Strategy Document

The minimum elements a NeoNephos Social Media Strategy Document should contain is the following:

* **Timeframe of validity**: It MUST be stated for how long the document is valid.
* **Reference to Vision Documents**: It MUST be mentioned which concrete foundational documents (e.g. NeoNephos Roadmap) have been used to derive the Social Media Strategy.
* **Success Criteria**: The Socia Media Strategy Document needs to have its own measurable success criteria (e.g. [SMART goals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SMART_criteria)).

### Format

Social Media Strategy Documents are always in markdown. The source of truth is the Social Media Strategy Document which resides in the Outreach Committee's repository.

## Lifecycle

A Social Media Strategy Document, once accepted, has a dedicated lifecycle. The change in lifecycle does not have to be actively voted upon but is implied  by the Social Media Document's timeframe of validity.

#### Active

A Social Media Strategy Document is active once it has been approved by the Outreach Committee *and* its indicated validity timeline starts.

#### Emeritus

A Social Media Strategy Document is in Emeritus once its indicated validity timeline expires, is superseded by another Social Media Strategy Document or by simple vote of the Outreach Committee or its Social Media SIG. Once in Emeritus, the Chairperson of the Social Media SIG (or a representative thereof) fills out the Submit Social Media Strategy Results Issue template [here](https://github.com/neonephos/outreach/issues).

##### Responsibilities

* Chairperson of the Social Media SIG (or a representative thereof) fills out [this form](https://github.com/neonephos/outreach/issues).

### Review Meeting

After conclusion of a Social Media Strategy Document (e.g. due to expiration) a Review Meeting in the Social Media SIG will be held.

This meeting addresses:

* Have the success criteria outlined in the Social Media Strategy Document been reached in the indicated period?
* What went well?
* What went wrong?
* Estimation of total costs spent in the endeavour of furthering the strategy outlined in the Social Media Strategy Document.

## Working with Third Parties

Third party services such as LF Marketing may create bodies of work whose scope fall under the purview of this policy. When working with such services, special care must be taken to ensure any policies and guidelines described in this policy are still adhered to. It is the responsibility of the Social Media SIG to ensure that work packages are properly described and resulting bodies of work sufficiently reviewed.
